# OpenHear Immediate Engagement List — 20 Research, Hardware, Academic, and Commercial Targets

These are the 20 most relevant research papers, open hardware projects, academic institutions, and commercial partners for OpenHear right now. Each entry states what it brings to the build.

---

## 1. Paul Bach-y-Rita et al. — tactile sensory substitution

**What it is.** Foundational sensory-substitution research showing that structured tactile input can be learned as another sensory modality.

**What it brings.** The conceptual and methodological base for OpenHear's aids-free somatosensory architecture.

---

## 2. Novich & Eagleman (2015) — vibrotactile encoding in space and time

**What it is.** A key paper on encoding complex information through vibrotactile patterns.

**What it brings.** Direct design guidance for dense wrist haptic mapping and temporal/spatial encoding decisions.

---

## 3. Reed et al. (2019) — phonemic tactile display for speech communication

**What it is.** A strong modern reference for tactile speech encoding on the arm.

**What it brings.** A starting point for OpenHear's phoneme sandbox, speech mapping, and adaptation metrics.

---

## 4. Perrotta et al. (2021) — deciphering sounds through vibration on the skin

**What it is.** Experimental evidence that people can learn sound structure through patterned vibration.

**What it brings.** A modern empirical base for the claim that richer tactile hearing substitution is trainable.

---

## 5. Neosensory — VEST and Buzz

**What it is.** The closest currently shipped comparator in auditory-to-haptic substitution.

**What it brings.** Learning-curve benchmarks, design lessons, and a clear target for OpenHear to exceed on channel density, scene intelligence, and sovereignty.

---

## 6. BrainPort (Wicab)

**What it is.** A regulatory precedent for sensory substitution as a mechanism distinct from conventional amplification.

**What it brings.** A model for regulatory positioning and evidence packaging of an aids-free OpenHear configuration.

---

## 7. Tympan

**What it is.** The strongest existing open-source hearing hardware platform in OpenHear's immediate orbit.

**What it brings.** Immediate prototyping hardware, open hearing signal-path lessons, and a bridge from current pipeline mode to wrist-native hardware.

---

## 8. OpenEarable (KIT / Karlsruhe)

**What it is.** An open ear-worn sensing and audio research platform.

**What it brings.** Useful contrast architecture, sensor-fusion lessons, and open wearable research patterns even though OpenHear's north star moves off the ear.

---

## 9. RISC-V International and OpenHW Group

**What it is.** The open ISA and reference-core ecosystem best suited to a hearing-specific NPU.

**What it brings.** License-clean silicon foundations, core IP, and a community review path from FPGA to ASIC.

---

## 10. Lattice ECP5 + Project Trellis + FOSSi Foundation

**What it is.** The most practical open FPGA toolchain path for deterministic prototype silicon work.

**What it brings.** A genuinely open validation route for the hearing NPU and real-time haptic scheduler.

---

## 11. UCL Ear Institute

**What it is.** One of the strongest UK centres for audiology and translational hearing research.

**What it brings.** Clinical study infrastructure, hearing science expertise, and a credible UK validation partner.

---

## 12. Manchester Centre for Audiology and Deafness (ManCAD)

**What it is.** A leading UK audiology and deafness research centre.

**What it brings.** Replication capacity, speech-in-noise expertise, and another clinical partner to avoid single-lab dependence.

---

## 13. Purdue Haptic Interface Research Laboratory / Hong Z. Tan

**What it is.** A major haptics research node with direct relevance to tactile speech and wearable perception.

**What it brings.** Psychophysics, encoding design, and experimental support for wrist bandwidth studies.

---

## 14. Stanford / Eagleman sensory-substitution lineage

**What it is.** The academic orbit most closely associated with modern auditory tactile substitution and cortical remapping evidence.

**What it brings.** A route to stronger neuroscience validation of OpenHear adaptation claims.

---

## 15. MIT Picower Institute / Li-Huei Tsai 40 Hz programme

**What it is.** The best-known research cluster around 40 Hz sensory stimulation and neural effects.

**What it brings.** The most credible early evidence base for OpenHear's therapeutic-frequency layer.

---

## 16. Whole-body vibration and vibroacoustic therapy literature cluster

**What it is.** The interdisciplinary evidence base around low-frequency vibration for regulation, recovery, and physiological modulation.

**What it brings.** Candidate protocols, contraindication frameworks, and a structured starting point for the 30-300 Hz programme.

---

## 17. Apple HealthKit, Apple Watch, Core Haptics, Core Audio, and Accessibility ecosystem

**What it is.** The most mature consumer stack for on-device health correlation, high-quality haptics, and accessibility automation.

**What it brings.** Immediate infrastructure for stress correlation, sleep scheduling, secondary haptic output, and native profile switching.

---

## 18. Android Health Connect, TensorFlow Lite, Oboe, Wear OS, and advanced haptics ecosystem

**What it is.** The broadest mobile open deployment surface for sovereign on-device sensing and ML.

**What it brings.** Lower-cost reach, open distribution options, and strong on-device runtime tools outside Apple's ecosystem.

---

## 19. bHaptics and the dense wearable haptics ecosystem

**What it is.** A commercial benchmark for multi-actuator wearable haptic systems.

**What it brings.** Real-world lessons in comfort, pattern authoring, driver design, and user comprehension at scale.

---

## 20. Sharp Hearing (US-based audiology clinic) — first formal industry contact, 20 April 2026

**What it is.** The first recorded formal US industry contact for OpenHear, already adjacent to haptic hearing products through its visibility of Neosensory Buzz.

**What it brings.** Real-world clinical feedback, patient-facing validation, US market context, and an immediate collaboration path between the architecture and field deployment.

---

7. Vibroacoustic and whole-body vibration reviews relevant to 30-300 Hz delivery
